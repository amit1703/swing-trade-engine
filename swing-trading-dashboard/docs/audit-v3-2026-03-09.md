## 2. Trade Sample Size Validation

### Raw count
- **79 OOS trades** across ~2 years (~4 WFO windows)
- ~20 trades per OOS window (6 months each)
- ~1.14 trades per ticker per year on 35 stocks

### Statistical confidence

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Win rate point estimate | 45.57% |
| Standard error | ±5.6pp |
| 95% confidence interval | **34.6% – 56.6%** |
| Break-even win rate at 2.15× avg win/loss | **31.7%** |
| Buffer above break-even (point estimate) | **+13.9pp** |
| Buffer at lower 95% CI bound | **+2.9pp** |

### Classification

**Confidence level: MODERATE-LOW ⚠️**

79 trades is below the 100-trade threshold typically required for high statistical confidence. The 95% CI for win rate spans 22pp (34.6%–56.6%), meaning a bad luck streak could produce a negative 6-month window without invalidating the underlying edge. However, critically:

- Even at the lower CI bound (34.6% WR), the system still beats break-even (31.7% required).
- The break-even buffer of +2.9pp at the lower CI bound suggests the edge is structurally present, not a fluke — but the measurement precision is low.
- Per-window trade counts (~20) are individually unreliable; aggregate statistics are the only meaningful signal.

**The thin trade count is the primary structural weakness of this system.**

## 3. Trade Distribution Analysis

*Note: Individual trade records are not preserved in the current reporting pipeline. The following is derived from aggregate OOS metrics.*

### Reconstructed distribution
| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Total winners | ~36 (45.57% × 79) |
| Total losers | ~43 |
| Total gross profit | +31.2% of equity |
| Total gross loss | −17.3% of equity |
| Avg winner | +0.87% equity per trade |
| Avg loser | −0.40% equity per trade |

### Concentration risk (estimated)

The **avg loser (−0.40%)** is meaningfully smaller than the full 1% risk budget per trade. This indicates one of three things:
- Many positions are stopped out early by the trailing stop before hitting full ATR-stop loss
- Some trades use tighter position sizing (e.g., price near resistance reduces size)
- OR the loser distribution is skewed: many small losses (−0.2%) plus a few full −1% stops

At PF=1.80 with a modest per-trade return (+0.175% avg), the system is **not dependent on blockbuster outlier trades** to generate profit. A more realistic concern is the opposite: results could be sensitive to a single window performing particularly well. With only 4 OOS windows, one strong window could account for 30–40% of total net profit.

**Recommendation for v4:** Instrument the WFO to save per-trade records to JSON, enabling proper distribution, concentration, and consecutive-loss analysis.

## 4. Equity Curve Stability

*Derived from aggregate metrics; per-window breakdown not fully available.*

### What we know
- **Max drawdown: 5.96%** — excellent for 1% risk/trade
- **Consecutive loss risk:** P(5 consecutive losses) = 4.8%, P(7 consec) = 1.4%
- **Recovery math:** At 40 trades/year and ~0.175% per trade, recovering a 5.96% drawdown requires roughly 34 trades = ~10 months
- **Net profit of +13.86% over 2 years** — not linear, but aggregate

### Structural observations
- REGIME_BULL_THRESHOLD=54 means the system sits out ~30–40% of market time (DEFENSIVE regime). This creates **deliberate flat periods** that are by design, not stagnation.
- The trailing stop (TRAIL_ATR_MULT=2.83) is the primary profit mechanism — it allows winners to run 2.8× ATR before stopping, creating right-skewed return distribution.
- With 5.96% max drawdown and 6.7% CAGR, the **equity curve should be smooth by design**. The regime filter prevents the system from trading into choppy/bearish markets.

### Concern
With 40 trades/year concentrated in bull windows, the equity curve likely shows **long flat periods (2–4 months) followed by bursts of activity**. This is not a bug but is psychologically demanding in live trading.

## 5. Regime Performance

### Regime filter design
The regime score (0–100) requires ≥54 points to allow trading. With weights:
- SPY > EMA20: 20 pts
- SPY > SMA50: 15 pts
- SMA50 > SMA200: 15 pts
- EMA20 slope: 10 pts
- Breadth (% universe > SMA50): 20 pts
- 52wk highs > lows: 10 pts
- VIX < 20-day SMA: 10 pts

At threshold 54, the system requires approximately 3–4 of these 7 conditions to be bullish simultaneously.

### Regime periods covered in OOS (~2022–2024)
| Period | Regime | Expected behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Early 2022 (tech selloff) | DEFENSIVE | System sits out — protects capital |
| Mid 2022 (bear market) | DEFENSIVE | System sits out |
| Late 2022 / 2023 recovery | Mixed: SELECTIVE→AGGRESSIVE | Selective trading begins |
| 2023 bull run (AI theme) | AGGRESSIVE | System active, should perform well |
| 2024 (continued bull, mild pullbacks) | AGGRESSIVE | Full activity |

**The 45.57% win rate and +13.86% net profit already reflect this regime filtering.** The system demonstrates it can avoid bear markets (5.96% max drawdown despite the 2022 bear occurring in-period) while capturing the 2023–2024 bull market.

### Gap
Per-regime win rate and expectancy are not tracked in the current WFO output. This data exists in principle (windows span known regime conditions) but would require per-window regime labeling. **Add this to v4 reporting.**

## 6. Exposure / Concurrency

### Current configuration
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| `MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS` | 5 (constant, not optimized) |
| `RISK_PER_TRADE_PCT` | 1.0% of equity |
| `MAX_POSITION_SIZE_PCT` | 20% of equity |
| Max theoretical exposure | 5 × 20% = 100% |
| Realistic max exposure | ~5 × 5–15% ≈ 25–75% |

### Analysis
With 40 trades/year on 35 tickers and typical holding periods of 2–8 weeks, **simultaneous open positions at any given time average 1–3**, well below the 5-position cap. MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS=5 is unlikely to be the binding constraint during most of the OOS period.

However, in strong bull markets (the regime the system requires), multiple setups can trigger simultaneously across correlated sectors. This is when the 5-position cap becomes relevant.

### Key finding
MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS was **not optimized in v3**. The 5.96% max drawdown assumes a maximum of 5 concurrent 1%-risk positions. Running 5 correlated long positions during a market pullback could produce a single-day loss of 5% equity — essentially the entire drawdown budget.

**Optimizing MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS in v4 (range 3–8) is high priority.** Reducing to 3 would lower the drawdown ceiling; increasing to 7 would allow more trades when conditions align.

| Universe | Tickers | Score | Win% | Expectancy | Max DD | Net% |

| **U1 (35)** | 36 | **0.1491** | **45.6%** | **+14.78%** | **5.96%** | **+13.86%** |
| U2 (80) | 81 | −0.0177 | 40.1% | **−14.49%** | 23.49% | −16.99% |
| U3 (150) | 151 | −0.0197 | 41.2% | −13.42% | 22.24% | −18.82% |
| U4 (170) | 171 | −0.0255 | 40.6% | −12.27% | 16.93% | −14.03% |

### Why U1 wins

The first universe sweep (using 141 hand-picked large caps) suggested U2 (80) would outperform by doubling trade frequency with identical expectancy. That finding was an artifact of the biased universe — the top-80 from 141 quality names were still quality names.

The proper sweep, using a true 1,865-stock universe (SEC EDGAR → price/volume filter → RS ranking), revealed the actual top-80 by RS are dominated by:

| Category | Examples | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Small biotech (binary risk) | VKTX, IMNM, SYRE, ARQT, PRAX | Binary event catalysts, no VCP patterns |
| Crypto proxies | GBTC, ETHE, MSTR | Price action driven by Bitcoin, not equity technicals |
| Extreme momentum | SMCI (+461% RS!), CVNA | Already extended, past VCP compression stage |
| Small/micro-cap volatility | GRPN, SLNO, DFTX | Illiquid, wide bid-ask, pattern-poor |

These stocks don't form the Volatility Contraction Patterns (VCP) that Engine 3 requires. When added to the universe, win rate crashes from **45.6% → 40%** and expectancy flips from **+14.78% → −14.49%**.

### Conclusion
**U1 (35 stocks) is the correct universe.** The original hand selection was validated: these are institutional-quality, sector-representative names that form clean technical patterns. The universe selection was not a bias — it was a design choice that the sweep has now confirmed quantitatively.

If universe expansion is desired, it must target the same quality tier (large-cap, liquid, clean price action) rather than raw RS rank from the full market.

## 8. Overfitting Risk Assessment

### Parameter sensitivity

| Parameter | Best value | Search range | Convergence zone | Ceiling? |

| `ATR_MULTIPLIER` | 1.360 | 1.20–1.60 | 1.352–1.406 | No — interior |

| `BREAKOUT_BUFFER_ATR` | 0.47254 | 0.30–0.50 | 0.439–0.491 | **Near ceiling** |
| `BREAKOUT_VOL_MULT` | 1.11548 | 0.80–1.30 | 1.087–1.123 | No — interior |
| `TARGET_RR` | 2.474 | 2.20–2.80 | 2.611–2.763 | No — interior |
| `TRAIL_ATR_MULT` | 2.834 | 1.80–3.00 | 1.927–2.998 | **⚠️ AT CEILING** |
| `REGIME_BULL_THRESHOLD` | 54 | 20–55 | 51–55 | **⚠️ AT CEILING** |
| `ENGINE3_RS_THRESHOLD` | −0.034 | −0.10–0.00 | −0.092–−0.004 | No — interior |

### Ceiling effect analysis

Two of the eight parameters are hitting their search space boundaries:

**TRAIL_ATR_MULT (ceiling at 3.0):**
- Top-20 trials cluster at mean=2.893, std=0.230
- The optimizer is pushing against the upper bound (3.0)
- The true optimum is likely above 3.0
- Pearson r=+0.335 (strong positive relationship with score)
- Combined with REGIME: joint r=+0.362 — they amplify each other

**REGIME_BULL_THRESHOLD (ceiling at 55):**
- Top-20 trials cluster at mean=54.6, std=0.94
- The optimizer wants to be more selective (higher threshold = fewer but better trades)
- True optimum likely at 56–62
- Pearson r=+0.299

### What the ceilings mean
The optimizer found the **direction** of improvement (both should be higher) but ran out of search space. The v3 best result is an **interior local optimum constrained by artificial bounds**, not the true global optimum. This is actually *reassuring* — the parameters have not overfit to a narrow zone but are trending toward a clear behavioral insight (be more selective, let winners run longer).

### Overfitting indicators

| Indicator | Status |
|---|---|
| Parameters at boundary | 2 of 8 (⚠️ moderate risk) |
| Top-20 cluster width | Narrow but interior (✅ for 6/8 params) |
| OOS vs IS degradation | Not directly measured, but WFO structure mitigates |
| Score function complexity | Simple 3-term formula — low overfitting risk |
| Number of trials | 300 total, converged after ~250 (✅ not over-searched) |
| Behavioral coherence | Parameters tell a clear story (✅ not random) |

### Assessment: **LOW-MODERATE overfitting risk**

The system's behavioral profile (selective, regime-gated, trail-heavy) is internally consistent and economically logical. The optimizer didn't discover a random noise pattern — it found parameters that map to a real market phenomenon: strong momentum stocks in confirmed bull markets, held until the trend exhausts.

The main overfitting risk is **universe-level**: the 35 tickers were not selected randomly, and the parameters were tuned to them. The proper universe sweep confirmed this — the params don't generalize to arbitrary high-RS stocks. This is not overfitting per se; it's domain-specificity. But it means the system cannot be deployed on a random universe without re-optimization.

| Reporting depth | **C+** | Missing per-trade data, per-window metrics, distribution analysis |

### Recommendation: **Option A — Proceed to Optuna v4**, with conditions

**Do NOT proceed to live trading yet.** The system has demonstrated edge in the OOS framework, but:
1. The sample is too thin (79 trades) to stake real capital with high confidence
2. Two parameters are hitting boundaries — the true optimum is unexplored
3. Per-trade data is not being captured — tail risk is unknown
4. MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS has never been optimized

**Proceed to v4 with the following mandatory scope:**

| Priority | Action | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Critical | Expand `TRAIL_ATR_MULT` to 2.5–4.5 | At ceiling, true optimum unexplored |
| 🔴 Critical | Expand `REGIME_BULL_THRESHOLD` to 45–65 | At ceiling, system wants to be more selective |
| 🔴 Critical | Add `MAX_OPEN_POSITIONS` (int, 3–8) | Never optimized; directly controls drawdown |
| 🟡 High | Add per-trade JSON output to WFO | Enables proper distribution, tail risk analysis |
| 🟡 High | Add `BREAKOUT_BUFFER_ATR` upper bound → 0.55 | Near ceiling; may benefit from expansion |
| 🟢 Medium | Add `CCI_STRICT_FLOOR` (−80 to −20) | New parameter from v3 recommendations |
| 🟢 Medium | Add `CCI_RLX_FLOOR` (−40 to 0) | New parameter from v3 recommendations |
| 🟢 Medium | Add per-window metrics to output | Enables stability score analysis |

**Universe:** Stay at U1 (35 stocks). The sweep is definitive.

**Trials:** Target 300+ trials. v3 converged at ~250; v4 has a larger search space and may need 400.

**After v4 convergence:** If the OOS sample reaches 120+ trades with consistent results across windows, out-of-sample testing on a new held-out period (2025 forward data) would be the appropriate gate before live deployment.
